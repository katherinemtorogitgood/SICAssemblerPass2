The hardest part of this assignment was figuring out how to correctly generate the M records. 
Understanding exactly when they were required, how the addresses needed to be calculated, 
and why they appear after the END directive took a lot of trial and error. Even small mistakes in the 
format caused the object files to fail the test cases, so getting the M record logic to match the expected 
output was more difficult than I expected.

Another major challenge was making sure the assembler handled all directives and text records properly. 
Managing BYTE, WORD, RESB, and RESW without breaking the text record boundaries required careful debugging,
especially when dealing with long sequences or gaps in memory. Keeping Pass 1 and Pass 2 consistent, and 
making sure the parsing and encoding worked together, also added complexity to the project. Even though it took 
time to fix everything, it helped me better understand how an assembler really works behind the scenes.
